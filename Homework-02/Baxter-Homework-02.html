Explain each term in 3.5.3f and what assumptions you need make to get to equation (1)¶

Term 1: Storage term. Change in mean potential temperature over change in time.

Term 2: Advective term. The redistribution of heat resulting from the mean winds.

Term 3: Sources and Sinks. The first is the diabatic heating term, which is representative of latent heat release. The second is the loss of heat through dissipation.

Term 4: Turbulent term. The eddy-driven redistibution of heat.

The assumptions made to get to Equation (1) and complete a simple growth rate model are:

1) The magnitude of the potential temperature jump (delta theta) remains constant as the boundary layer grows.

2) The rate of entrainment of warmer air from aloft is a fixed and known fraction of the surface heat flux. By this we can use the empirically-derived relationship that the heat flux at the top of the boundary layer can be constrained to approximately 20% of the surface flux and this gives us alpha.

4) How sensitive is the final BL height to the initial height h(0)? You may determine this sensitivity either analytically or computationally.¶

The final boundary layer height is inversely proportional to the initial height because the growth in the boundary layer depends on how efficiently the heat flux can be mixed within the mixed layer, and this decreases with a deeper boundary layer. The effects of the initial boundary layer height on the final boundary layer height are primarily seen in the earlier time steps, where a shallow boundary layer will have a large initial increase with a large surface heat flux, whereas a deep boundary layer will have a muted response to a large surface heat flux. The change in boundary layer height increases exponentially with the initial height, this is because there is a h(t-1)^2 in the equation. The final boundary layer potential temperature increases linearly with increasing initial height, as can be seen from the linear equation determining it. A lot of the increase seems to happen in the first time step, where it is possible the boundary layer, being very shallow, is somewhat more susceptible to warming or deepening.

def calc_conv_BL_f(ho,gamma=2):
    h = np.zeros(13)
    h[0] = ho
    theta = np.zeros(13)
    theta[0] = 20
    for t in range(1,13):
        h[t] = np.sqrt(((2*1.2*12)*Hmax/(-gamma*rho*Cp*np.pi))*(np.cos(np.pi*t/12) - np.cos(np.pi*(t-1)/12))+h[t-1]*h[t-1])
        theta[t] = gamma*h[t] + theta[0] + gamma*h[0]
    return h[-1],theta[-1]
